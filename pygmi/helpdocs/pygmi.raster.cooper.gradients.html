Gradient Calculation

The gradient calculation calculates the devrivative of the data in the azimuth specified. It allows for the calcutaion of a conventional horizontal derivative, a derivative ratio (ratio of x and y derivatives), a vertical derivative and a total horizontal derivative.

Options

  • Azimuth - Available for both calculations. This is the direction of the derivative, in the plane of the data. It coincides with the direction of the features being highlighted, where 0 degrees is E-W, 45 degrees is NE-SW, -45 degrees is NW-SE and 90 degrees is N-S.
  • Strength Factor - This is the strength of the derivative ratio. A value of 1 is recommended as a starting point.
